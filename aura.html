How it works

See Aura in action with screenshots & functionalities

Install Aura on your router

You can also buy our pre-packaged router



Register & enable sharing

Aura creates an open wifi on top of your password protected wifi to which Aura users could connect to



Set aside monthly sharing data

If you have 100 GB data & you typically use 50-60 GB every month, you may like to set aside 30GB for wifi sharing.
Sharing is automatically disabled when quota limit for the month is hit



Your router is well protected

Sharing wifi through Aura is safe. Other Aura users are on different subnet & could not even access your router admin page.



Built in QoS tools to ensure higher privilege to you

Aura intelligently throttles other connected Aura users' bandwidth to ensure you always get acceptable speed
In worst case, you can always force disconnect other Aura users or disable sharing completely.



Accumulate Karma GBs & seamlessly sell to users who need some

When an Aura users is using your wifi, his Karma GBs are transferring to you. Any other user who is out of Karma GBs can buy some from you. You can use your Karma GBs to connect to any other Aura wifi. Its like a virtual currency.



Use your accumulated Karma GBs with others' Aura wifi

Next time when you work from your favorite cafe, before tethering your phone 3G, check if an Aura wifi is available & use it.
Its lot faster & cheaper.



Aura wifis are better than open wifi hotspots

Your Aura credentials work everywhere & hence you do not need to register or give out your details at every new hotspot.
Aura is built on top of openwrt, an open source router OS & perform lot better than lot of other hotspot softwares that are closed source, built by a small dev shop & which does not work half the time



See connection speed of an Aura wifi upfront

You can see the real speed you getting from Aura wifi prior to connecting & anytime while you are connected.



Aura wifi are phishing safe

Its easy for a hacker to create an open wifi, name it Aura & phish your Aura credentials when you connect & login to them.
You set a secret statement while you register at Aura which is shown to you when you put in your Aura username while connecting to an Aura wifi. A hacker will not know your secret statement.
